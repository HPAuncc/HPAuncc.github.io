About Hampton Abbott

High-achieving Data Science student and Chancellor's List honoree at UNC Charlotte with experience in data pipelines, predictive maintenance, and customer analytics. Applies data-driven methods to analyze real-world problems and improve operations.

What I bring:

  • NFL Home-Field Advantage. Analyzed 6,175 games (2000–2023) with Python, pandas, and nflverse — finding a 56.26% home win rate.
  • Business & Data Experience. 10+ years applying analytics across fleet management, e-commerce, events, and residential services.
  • IBM Certified. IBM Data Science & Applied Data Science professional certificates (March 2025).
  • Full Stack of Tools. Python · SQL · Java · pandas · NumPy · matplotlib · seaborn · Git · Jupyter.
  • Data Ethics Focus. Writing at the intersection of sports analytics, digital privacy, and social responsibility.

I am a Sports Analytics major at UNC Charlotte with a deep-rooted passion for the statistics that drive the games we love. While my technical focus is on optimizing performance and strategy in sports, I am equally driven by a passion for digital privacy. I believe that as we collect more data—on the field and off—we have a growing responsibility to handle that information ethically. My goal this semester is to merge these interests, exploring how high-level analytics can coexist with robust data privacy standards.

Key Finding: Home-Field Advantage is Real — and Declining

"Across 6,175 regular-season games from 2000–2023, home teams won 56.26% of the time. The advantage is real — but it appears weaker than it was in the early 2000s, and 2020 stands out as the most extreme outlier (49.80%)."
